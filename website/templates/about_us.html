More About Us

We are 3 student athletes who want to bring awareness to student athletes mental health. This is something that is not talked about enough, and we need start discussing this and coming together as a community. Whether you are struggling mentally due to your own pressure, your coach, your teammates, your parents, your school work, racism, an injury, or whether you're a parent that feels helpless in supporting your child through all of this, we want this to be a safe space to talk about this. We hope that by talking about these experiences openly, we can help support one another and decrease the amount of suicides that occur at college with student athletes. By talking about this and having these uncomfortable conversation, we are forced to have changes made and bring awareness to this topic. We want this to be anonymous so everyone feels comfortable sharing their story and knowing that they are not alone.

Mental health refers to your emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It is important for athletes because mental health plays a significant role in an athlete's performance and overall well-being. Good mental health helps athletes to cope with stress, build resilience, and maintain positive relationships.

Some common mental health issues that athletes may face include anxiety, depression, eating disorders, addiction, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). These issues can arise from factors such as performance pressure, injury, or the transition to retirement.

Some signs that an athlete may be struggling with mental health issues include changes in behavior, mood, or sleep patterns, a decline in performance, social withdrawal, and increased substance use.

Strategies for managing stress and anxiety as an athlete include setting realistic goals, practicing relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation, getting adequate sleep and nutrition, and seeking social support.

Resources available for athletes seeking help for their mental health include athletic trainers, team doctors, sports psychologists, and mental health hotlines or online counseling services.
